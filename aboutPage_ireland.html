How Ireland Measure Poverty

There are different ways to understand poverty. In Ireland, “absolute poverty” refers to situations in which a person lacks basic necessities such as food and shelter. And to be “at risk of poverty” reflects the concept of “relative poverty”, which applies when a household’s income is below 60% of the national median (2023 it was 10.6%). Ireland is aligned with the United Nations’ 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and working to analyse data to create new social and economic strategies.
