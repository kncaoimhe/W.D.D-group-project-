Understanding Poverty in Switzerland

Despite Switzerland's wealth, poverty is rising because many people have a poor income and cannot fully take part in normal daily life. More than 700,000 people in Switzerland are affected by poverty, and many others live only slightly above the poverty line. These individuals often struggle to cover basic daily needs and must rely on different kinds of support.

However, poverty is not only about money. It also affects People's opportunities, confidence, and ability to take part in society. For this reason, Caritas projects focus on more than financial aid. They help people stay socially connected, take part in community life, and regain a sense of control over their own future.

Groups at Risk of Poverty

  • Single adults under 65
  • Families with 3 or more children
  • Single-parent households
  • People with low education levels
  • Older adults (65+), especially if living alone
