Understanding Poverty in Switzerland

Despite Switzerland’s reputation for wealth and stability, poverty is steadily increasing. Many people earn too little to keep up with the rising cost of living and cannot fully participate in everyday life. Today, more than 700,000 people in Switzerland are affected by poverty, and many others live only just above the poverty line. They often struggle to cover basic needs such as food, housing, and healthcare, and must rely on various forms of support to get by. Poverty, however, is not only about lacking money. It also limits people’s opportunities, affects their well-being, and can lead to social isolation. In a wealthy society, this isolation can feel especially harsh, leaving individuals with a weakened sense of control and confidence.

That is why Caritas focuses on more than financial help. Their projects aim to strengthen people both socially and emotionally, helping them stay connected, take part in community life, and rebuild trust in their own abilities. By offering guidance, community spaces, and educational opportunities, Caritas helps people regain stability and a sense of dignity in shaping their future.

Switzerland's 10 Goals to Reduce Poverty by 2030

  • Everyone has enough for basic needs
  • People at risk get help early
  • Social support stays strong
  • Jobs pay fairly
  • Children grow up without poverty
  • Affordable housing for all
  • Help is available in tough situations
  • Equal chances across the country
  • Migrants are supported to integrate
  • A strong and fair economy
