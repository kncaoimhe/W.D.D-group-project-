Facts about India

  • The population in India is 1.3 billion people. It is the seventh largest country in the world. The capital of India is New Delhi.
  • It is the world's second most populous country. The most populous and cosmopolitan cities in the world are Mumbai, Kolkata & Delhi. The world's fastest growing high technology centers are Bangalore, Chennai & Hyderabad.
  • India has 22 major languages and many more dialects. The official languages in India is English & Hindi.
  • The national animal is the tiger, the national bird is the peacock, and the national flower is the lotus.
  • The iconic Taj Mahal is one of the 7 Wonders of the World. It was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983.
  • The currency used in India is called Indian Rupees (INR).

Facts about sustainability in India

  • India ranks 109th in the global SDG Index with a score of 63.99%, showing steady improvement since 2020–21 and a strong Spillover Score of 95.74%,reflecting its positive global impact. As a leader in renewable energy,particularly solar and wind, and host of the International Solar Alliance, India has advanced sustainability while facing challenges from pollution linked to rapid economic growth. Traditional practices rooted in harmony with nature, rising consumer interest in recycling and device trade-ins, and government initiatives highlight progress, yet many efforts remain fragmented. Moving forward,greater collaboration, stronger infrastructure, and inclusive distribution of benefits will be essential to balance growth with environmental responsibility and ensure long-term sustainable development.

India & The UN 2030 Agenda Against Poverty

  • National integration: India has embedded all 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) into its national development agenda, guided by the principle of “no one left behind.” Subnational monitoring: The SDG India Index, developed by NITI Aayog and the UN, tracks state and union territory performance across social, economic, and environmental dimensions.Cooperative federalism: Progress toward SDGs is driven by collaboration between central, state, and local governments.
  • South-South cooperation: India shares knowledge and builds capacity with other developing nations to advance global SDG implementation.Poverty and hunger reduction: Schemes like Pradhan Mantri MUDRA Yojana provide credit to small businesses, creating jobs and improving livelihoods (Goals 1 & 2).Economic growth and employment: Initiatives such as Make in India and Start-up India foster manufacturing and entrepreneurship, supporting Goal 8. Health and well-being: Public health programs, including expansion of high-speed internet for service access, advance Goal 3.
  • Climate action: India targets 500 GW of non-fossil fuel energy capacity by 2030, aligning with Goal 13. Infrastructure development: Projects like Bharat Broadband Network Ltd enhance rural connectivity and promote resilient infrastructure.Global context and progress: India ranked 99th of 167 countries in the 2025 UN SDG Report; despite challenges, it shows positive trends in employment, reduced urban unemployment, and rising rural women’s self-employment.

Summary:

  • Progress & Rankings: India ranked 99th in the 2025 UN SDG Report with a score of 63.99%, and its Spillover Score of 95.74% reflects positive global influence.
  • Clean Energy Leadership: India is a global leader in solar and wind energy, hosts the International Solar Alliance, and aims for 500 GW of non‑fossil fuel energy capacity by 2030.
  • Inclusive Development: All 17 SDGs are integrated into India’s national agenda, supported by the SDG India Index, cooperative federalism, and South‑South cooperation.
  • Growth vs. Sustainability: Economic growth has reduced poverty but worsened pollution; cultural traditions emphasize sustainability, while consumer awareness in recycling and device trading is rising.
  • Targeted Programs & Empowerment: Anti‑poverty schemes address jobs, health, climate action, and rural infrastructure, with rural self‑employment—especially among women—boosting empowerment and poverty reduction.
