Poverty and Financial Struggles in Switzerland, 2018

In 2018, Switzerland remained one of the richest countries in Europe, but about 7.9% of the population, or roughly 660,000 people, were income poor. This means their earnings were too low to cover basic needs such as food, housing, and daily expenses. Many others who were not officially poor still faced financial difficulties. For example, 1 in 8 people had trouble paying bills, and around 20% could not afford an unexpected cost of CHF 2,500. Some people with jobs were still poor, showing that having work does not always protect against money problems. Many families could not afford a holiday or lived in homes with issues like noise, dampness, or safety concerns. Although long-term poverty is uncommon in Switzerland, these numbers show that even in a wealthy country, a significant number of people face real financial challenges and stress.

Poverty and Living Standards in Switzerland, 2021

In 2021, about 5.2% of people in Switzerland, or 448,000 people, could not afford important goods, services, or social activities because of money problems. Many could not pay for unexpected costs, replace old furniture, or go on a holiday. The poverty rate was 8.7%, which means these people earned less than the basic income needed for living. People most affected were unemployed, low-income workers, single parents, and some foreigners. Even some people with jobs struggled to make ends meet. Despite this, Switzerland still has one of the highest living standards in Europe.

Income Poverty in Switzerland, 2023

In 2023, based on income from 2022, about 8.1% of the Swiss population lived below the poverty line. People most affected included those living alone, single parents with children, people without further education, and households where no one works. Even some people with jobs earned very little and struggled to cover their basic needs. Many households had difficulty paying bills, buying essential items, or participating in social activities due to money problems. Despite these challenges, Switzerland continues to be one of the richest countries in Europe, with a high overall standard of living, but these numbers show that financial difficulties still affect a significant part of the population.

Summary

  • Switzerland is rich, but many people still face money problems.
  • About 7–8% of the population lived in income poverty in 2018–2023.
  • Many struggled to pay bills or afford unexpected costs.
  • Even people with jobs could be poor.
  • Single parents, unemployed people, low-income workers, and some foreigners were most affected.
  • Living standards are high overall, but financial stress is common for many.
